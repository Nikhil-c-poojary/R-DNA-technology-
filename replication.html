DNA Replication

DNA replication is the fundamental biological process by which a cell produces an identical copy of its DNA before cell division. It is a semi-conservative process, meaning each newly formed DNA molecule consists of one parental strand and one newly synthesized strand. Replication begins at a specific site called the origin of replication (Ori), where the DNA double helix unwinds to form a replication fork. Enzymes such as DNA helicase, primase, DNA polymerase, and ligase coordinate to synthesize new strands in the 5′→3′ direction. The leading strand is synthesized continuously, while the lagging strand is synthesized discontinuously as Okazaki fragments. DNA replication ensures accurate transmission of genetic information from one generation of cells to the next and is essential for growth, repair, and reproduction.
