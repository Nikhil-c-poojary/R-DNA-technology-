The Central Dogma Explorer refers to a structured way of understanding and visualizing the classical flow of genetic information in living systems. It builds on well-established principles while supporting modern learning needs. Core Concept Genetic information flows in a unidirectional manner: DNA → RNA → Protein Key Modules Replication (DNA → DNA) Accurate duplication of genetic material before cell division, ensuring continuity of life. Transcription (DNA → RNA) Synthesis of RNA (mRNA, tRNA, rRNA) from a DNA template, mediated by RNA polymerase. Translation (RNA → Protein) Decoding of mRNA at the ribosome to synthesize polypeptides using tRNA and amino acids. Extended Insights Exceptions and extensions: reverse transcription (RNA → DNA), RNA editing, and regulatory RNAs. Regulation at transcriptional, post-transcriptional, and translational levels. Purpose A Central Dogma Explorer is typically used as an educational or interactive framework to: Strengthen conceptual clarity for exams and coursework Visually connect molecular processes Bridge classical molecular biology with modern biotechnology applications
